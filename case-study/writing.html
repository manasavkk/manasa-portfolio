Why I do this

I just love writing and doing journalism has been a dream of mine for a while. This way I also get to see corners of campus and explore events I may not have explored otherwise!

What I have learnt

  • Brevity and structure. As someone that primarily does creative writing, cutting the editorializing is *hard*.
  • The Diamondback prohibits AI use of any kind in the writing process, including for improving sentence structure and grammar, which I learn the hard way after an article of mine I spent hours interviewing and writing for was scrapped. This was hard to adapt to initially as I have become a regular user of such tools. However, it has been a great feeling to write everything on my own. I realised that I have been slowly abandoning the craft of the process, and it feels good to be deliberate and intentional about each word with my work.
  • I have also lost any sense of awkwardness with randomly approaching people for an interview and have grown a thicker skin to rejections and awkward pauses. Holding structured interviews and planning out pointed questions backed by research remains my favourite though.
