CS & Math Tutoring

Academic Success & Tutorial Services, UMD · Feb 2025 — Present
TL;DR: Tutored 10+ students a week in Computer Science and Mathematics topics. I would recap student's lecture content the week before, sometimes provided teaching material, adapted my teaching style to how individual students understood, and cared a lot about making the material stick.

What I did

I tutored students one-on-one and in small groups through UMD's Academic Success & Tutorial Services. I taught Object Oriented Programming I and II, Calculus I & II, advanced algorithms and Discrete Mathematics across semesters, teaching at least one CS and Math class each semester. These courses often had higher rates for student failing or dropping them.

I practiced adaptive teaching by figuring out whether someone is stuck on the concept, the notation, or just the pacing of the course and adjusting my teaching styleaccordingly.

Why I did it

Teaching forces you to actually understand something. You cannot fake your way through explaining why a greedy algorithm fails on a specific class of inputs to someone who will immediately ask a follow-up. It's made me a more precise and patient thinker.

It also matters as A lot of students in these "weed out" courses decide whether they stay in CS based on how these classes go. Being part of keeping that door open for someone was an important and meaningful experience.
